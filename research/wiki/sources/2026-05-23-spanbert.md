# SpanBERT: Improving Pre-training by Representing and Predicting Spans

Joshi et al., UW + Princeton + FAIR — arXiv [1907.10529](https://arxiv.org/abs/1907.10529) — Code: [github.com/facebookresearch/SpanBERT](https://github.com/facebookresearch/SpanBERT)

## Abstract (paraphrased)

BERT's masked-language-modeling objective masks individual sub-word tokens. Many NLP tasks (QA, coreference, NER) actually reason about **spans** — so why not pretrain on spans? SpanBERT changes two things: (1) mask **contiguous random spans** instead of single tokens, (2) add a **Span Boundary Objective (SBO)** that forces the model to predict the entire content of a masked span using only the representations of the two boundary tokens. Result: same architecture and data as BERT-large, but consistently better on span-selection tasks. SQuAD 1.1: 94.6 F1. SQuAD 2.0: 88.7 F1. OntoNotes coref: 79.6 F1 (+6.6 over prior SOTA).

## Key findings

- **Span masking >> token masking.** Sample span lengths from `Geo(p=0.2)` clipped at 10, mean = 3.8 words. Mask ~15% of tokens total.
- **The SBO trick.** For a span (x_s, ..., x_e), predict each masked token `x_i` from ONLY `(x_{s-1}, x_{e+1}, p_{i-s+1})` — left boundary, right boundary, relative position embedding. Implemented as a 2-layer FFN with GeLU + LayerNorm. Loss is summed with MLM.
- **Single-sequence training beats NSP.** Removing Next Sentence Prediction entirely and training on single contiguous segments of up to 512 tokens consistently helps downstream tasks. Contradicts BERT's original ablation.
- **Random spans ≈ linguistic spans.** Masking random geometric spans is competitive with or better than masking named entities or noun phrases. The structure is learned, not imposed.
- **Cross-task generalization.** Gains on QA, coref, RE, and even GLUE. Span-aware pretraining doesn't hurt non-span tasks.

## Training details

- **Architecture:** BERT-large (24 layers, 1024 hidden, 16 heads, 340M params). Cased Wordpiece tokenizer.
- **Corpus:** BooksCorpus + English Wikipedia (same as BERT).
- **Steps:** 2.4M with batch size 256, max length 512. Adam(W) with lr 1e-4 warmup over 10k steps, linear decay.
- **SBO head:** 200-dim position embedding for relative position within masked span.
- **Hardware:** 32x V100 for 15 days.

## How this relates to our work

We use **BERTimbau** as the v3 backbone. BERTimbau uses standard BERT pretraining (MLM + NSP) with **whole-word masking**, not span masking. Implication: BERTimbau's boundary-token representations are *not* span-aware in the SpanBERT sense.

This matters for [[../questions/bioes-vs-span-based]]:

1. Our [[../questions/person-detection-failure-modes]] (compound names, names with titles) is exactly the kind of span-selection failure SpanBERT was designed to fix. Person spans tend to be 2-5 tokens long — well within SpanBERT's geometric distribution.

2. Our CPF/CNPJ/card-number fragmentation issue might benefit from SBO-style pretraining: if the boundary subwords (`680.` and `97`) encoded the whole span, classification would be more stable across sub-tokens.

3. SpanBERT validates that **the backbone matters as much as the fine-tuning architecture.** Our BIOES-vs-span-based debate may be partly moot if our backbone isn't span-aware.

## What we can actually do with this

Three options ranked by cost:

1. **Add an auxiliary SBO-style head during fine-tuning.** Cheap. Train v3.5 to jointly predict (a) per-token BIOES labels and (b) reconstruct masked entity content from span boundaries. Could improve span coherence without changing backbone or decoder.

2. **Continual pretraining of BERTimbau with span masking + SBO.** Medium cost (~50-200h GPU on PT corpus). Would yield "SpanBERTimbau". Closest direct application of the paper.

3. **Switch backbone to a span-pretrained model.** No public PT SpanBERT exists. Multilingual SpanBERT-like models: none well-supported. Would have to bridge through English.

## Concerns / open questions for us

- **SpanBERT is pretrained on English only.** No public PT or multilingual SpanBERT. Anyone training a PT version? Need to check.
- **Paper is 2019.** RoBERTa, DeBERTa-v3 came later. Is SBO still SOTA, or has it been superseded by other span-aware objectives?
- **BERTimbau's whole-word masking** is the closest thing we already have — partially captures span info. Marginal gain from SBO unclear without empirical test.
- **Geometric distribution mean = 3.8 words.** Most BR PII spans are within this range, but CPF/CNPJ in raw form (`123.456.789-00`) tokenize to 5-8 subwords. Need to check span length distribution in our dataset.

## Comparison with GLiNER (already ingested)

| | SpanBERT | GLiNER |
| --- | --- | --- |
| What changes | **Pretraining objective** | **Fine-tuning architecture** |
| Backbone | BERT-large | DeBERTa-v3 (any size) |
| Adds new types? | No, fixed at fine-tune | Yes, zero-shot via prompt |
| Span enumeration | Implicit (boundary encoding) | Explicit (K=12 enumerated) |
| Decoding | Same as BERT downstream | Span-matching + threshold |
| Year | 2019 | 2023 |
| Relevance to v4 | Backbone improvement | Architecture replacement |

They're complementary, not competing. Ideal v4 would be GLiNER-style decoder on a SpanBERT-style backbone (or DeBERTa-v3 which is already strong on spans for a different reason — disentangled attention).

## Open thread → BR-specific question

**Does BERTimbau's whole-word masking close most of the gap?**

If the SBO gain over WWM in SpanBERT's ablation is small, we don't need SpanBERTimbau. If it's large, the case for continual pretraining strengthens. Action: pull SpanBERT's Table 6 ablation. Result:

```
                           SQuAD2.0  Coref  TriviaQA
Subword Tokens (BERT)         83.8    77.7    76.3
Whole Words                   84.3    76.6    77.1
Named Entities                84.8    75.6    78.7
Noun Phrases                  85.0    76.7    77.7
Geometric Spans (SpanBERT)    85.4    76.4    78.8
```

WWM is **+0.5 SQuAD, -1.1 coref, +0.8 TriviaQA** over subword tokens. Geometric spans add another +1.1 SQuAD, -0.2 coref, +1.7 TriviaQA over WWM. So span masking adds something but the increment is modest **without SBO**. The big jump from full SpanBERT is from SBO + single-sequence training combined.

**Implication:** raw span masking alone is not the killer feature. The SBO objective is. If we do continual pretraining, SBO is what to focus on.
