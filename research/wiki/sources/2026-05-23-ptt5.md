# PTT5: Pretraining and validating the T5 model on Brazilian Portuguese data

Carmo et al., UNICAMP + NeuralMind — arXiv [2008.09144](https://arxiv.org/abs/2008.09144) — Code: [github.com/unicamp-dl/PTT5](https://github.com/unicamp-dl/PTT5)

> **Note:** This was originally fetched while searching for BERTimbau (the reading list had a hallucinated arXiv ID). PTT5 is from the **same UNICAMP/NeuralMind group** as BERTimbau and provides relevant context for PT-BR pretraining decisions. The actual BERTimbau paper (Souza, Nogueira, Lotufo 2020) was published at BRACIS and may not have an arXiv preprint at all.

## Abstract (paraphrased)

PT-BR NLP has a data and model gap relative to English. The authors pretrain T5 on **BrWac** (a large PT-BR web corpus), evaluating on sentence similarity, entailment, and headline generation tasks. They show that **PT-vocab + PT-pretrained T5 beats both vanilla English T5 and multilingual mT5** on PT-BR tasks. Public model: `unicamp-dl/ptt5-base-portuguese-vocab` on HF.

## Key findings

- **PT vocab matters as much as PT pretraining.** Using English T5 vocabulary with PT pretraining: better than English-only, worse than PT-vocab + PT-pretraining.
- **BrWac is the standard PT corpus.** ~2.5B tokens, web-scraped, used by BERTimbau, PTT5, and most PT models.
- **Multilingual models (mT5) underperform monolingual.** Confirmed across all 3 tasks. Tracks with similar findings for BERTimbau vs mBERT.
- **No NER eval.** They test sentence similarity (ASSIN, ASSIN2) and headline generation. NER not in scope.
- **Small param counts.** PTT5-base = 220M params. T5 generative model, not encoder-only.

## How this relates to our work

We use BERTimbau (also UNICAMP/NeuralMind, same group). PTT5 reinforces a few things we already implicitly rely on:

1. **PT-BR monolingual pretraining outperforms multilingual.** Justifies BERTimbau over mBERT for our v3.
2. **BrWac is the corpus to look at if we ever do continual pretraining.** Whether for SpanBERT-style ([[../sources/2026-05-23-spanbert]]) or LUKE-style ([[../sources/2026-05-23-luke]]) PT-BR adaptation.
3. **NeuralMind has PT-BR encoder + seq2seq stack.** BERTimbau (encoder), PTT5 (encoder-decoder). If we ever need a generative LM for the [[../concepts/llm-based-pii-detection]] fallback layer that we can run locally, PTT5 is a candidate.

## What we can actually use

- **BrWac as the corpus for any PT-BR continual pretraining.** If we want to make a "SpanBERTimbau" or "LUKE-BR", BrWac is where we start.
- **NeuralMind's HF org as a source of PT-BR models.** [huggingface.co/neuralmind](https://huggingface.co/neuralmind) hosts BERTimbau, PTT5, and others. If they release a span-aware or entity-aware PT model in future, that's where it'll show up.
- **PTT5 as a generative fallback option.** For a hypothetical "if NER says low-confidence, ask a local LM to verify" path. Smaller than mT5, locally deployable.

## Concerns / caveats

- **Not a NER paper.** Doesn't help our specific gap directly. Useful as context for backbone-shopping.
- **2020 paper.** Newer PT models exist (Albertina, mDeBERTa). Worth surveying current state separately.
- **No SOTA claim.** They show "better than mT5 and English T5" but didn't compete on a single dominant PT benchmark.

## Open questions
