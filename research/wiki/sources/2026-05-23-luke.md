# LUKE: Deep Contextualized Entity Representations with Entity-aware Self-attention

Yamada et al., Studio Ousia + RIKEN + UW + NAIST + NII — arXiv [2010.01057](https://arxiv.org/abs/2010.01057) — Code: [github.com/studio-ousia/luke](https://github.com/studio-ousia/luke)

## Abstract (paraphrased)

Standard transformer encoders (BERT, RoBERTa) treat entities as sequences of word tokens, with no special status. LUKE adds **entities as first-class tokens** alongside words, pre-trains them via a Wikipedia-derived masked-entity objective, and introduces **entity-aware self-attention** (separate query matrices for word→word, word→entity, entity→word, entity→entity attention). Achieves SOTA on Open Entity (typing), TACRED (relation), **CoNLL-2003 (NER, 94.3 F1)**, ReCoRD (QA), and SQuAD 1.1.

## Key findings

- **Entities as tokens beats entities-as-word-spans.** When the encoder explicitly represents entities, downstream entity tasks benefit. CoNLL-2003 NER: RoBERTa 92.4 → LUKE 94.3 F1 (+1.9 absolute).
- **Entity-aware self-attention** is the architectural novelty: four query matrices (Q_w2w, Q_w2e, Q_e2w, Q_e2e) instead of one. Same compute cost at inference (only training is slightly more expensive).
- **Wikipedia hyperlinks = free entity annotations.** Pretrained on entity-linked Wikipedia (500K entity vocabulary), MLM + masked-entity-prediction joint loss.
- **355M params (RoBERTa-large) + 128M entity embeddings = 483M total.** Bigger than BERT-base, on par with RoBERTa-large.
- **Ablations confirm both contributions matter.** Removing entity inputs: CoNLL-03 94.3 → 92.9 (-1.4). Removing entity-aware attention: more lift on relation/QA tasks than NER.
- **Extra pretraining alone doesn't explain the gains.** Training RoBERTa for 200K extra steps on Wikipedia: 92.4 → 92.5 only. LUKE's entity-aware design is doing real work.

## How this relates to our work

We use BERTimbau (PT-BR BERT) as v3 backbone — no entity awareness, standard MLM + NSP pretraining. LUKE shows that **architectural awareness of entities** during pretraining yields +1.9 F1 on NER, on top of a strong baseline. This is bigger than what GLiNER claims via span-based decoding alone.

Implications:
1. **LUKE is English-only.** No multilingual or PT-BR variant exists. Pretraining LUKE-PT would require an entity-linked PT-BR Wikipedia subset + significant compute (~similar to BERTimbau training cost).
2. **The "entities as tokens" pattern is portable.** We could imagine a future v4 backbone where, during fine-tuning, we explicitly mark BR PII categories as "candidate entity slots" and apply entity-aware attention.
3. **Hyperlinks-as-annotations is the magic trick.** Wikipedia PT has a smaller but non-trivial entity-linked subset. If someone trained LUKE-PT, it'd compete with BERTimbau on NER tasks.

## What we can actually use

- **Span enumeration + per-span entity classifier (NER setup in §4.3).** They enumerate all spans up to 16 words, classify each with the corresponding entity representation. **This is GLiNER-like decoding before GLiNER existed.** See [[../concepts/span-based-ner]]. Confirms the approach is robust across architectures.
- **Greedy non-overlapping span selection.** Same as GLiNER. Industry-standard for span-based NER decoding.
- **Initialize task-specific entity embeddings from `[MASK]` entity.** Useful trick if we ever introduce LUKE-style entity tokens for new PII categories at fine-tune time.

## Concerns / caveats

- **No PT-BR. No multilingual.** Direct adoption blocked.
- **Wikipedia entity coverage is biased.** Famous people, places, organizations. BR PII (CPFs, addresses of regular people) isn't in Wikipedia — LUKE's prior is for "entities that have Wikipedia articles", not "entities matching a regex pattern".
- **Inference overhead.** Adding ~5-15 entity tokens per sentence slightly increases compute. Acceptable for our latency budget (<100ms).
- **Span enumeration cost.** O(n² × K) for sentences of length n with max span K=16. Manageable for short BR documents, costly for long ones.
