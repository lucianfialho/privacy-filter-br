# How good is our synthetic dataset, really?

## What we have

54k synthetic examples generated by:
1. **4devs API** for valid BR PII (CPF/CNPJ/RG with checksum)
2. **Jinja templates** (18 BR document types — NF, contrato, holerite, etc + 4 extras for OAI categories)
3. **LLM rewriting** (gpt-5-nano with reasoning_effort=minimal) to make text more natural
4. **Auto-labeling** by string-matching the inserted PII back into the rewritten text

## Known weak spots

- **Same 10k profiles reused ~5x** to fill 54k examples. Limited entity diversity.
- **gpt-5-nano rewriting bias.** The model has stylistic tendencies (formality, structure) that don't match real BR documents.
- **No noise.** No OCR errors, no typos, no informal abbreviations ("cpf:" vs "CPF").
- **Templates are pristine.** Real NF/extrato/contrato have headers, footers, page breaks, table glitches that our templates don't simulate.

## Hypotheses

- **H1:** Our holdout F1 0.9934 overestimates real-world F1 by 10-20%.
- **H2:** Bias from gpt-5-nano rewriting is the biggest source of distribution shift, not the 4devs PII.
- **H3:** Adding 5-10k examples with controlled noise (OCR-like) would generalize better than adding 50k more clean examples.

## Open questions

1. What's the literature on **measuring** synthetic-to-real gap in NER?
2. Are there established protocols for **noise injection** in NER training data?
3. Can we do **distant supervision** on real BR documents (web-crawled NFs, public contratos, etc) without manual labeling?
4. **Counterfactual augmentation:** swap entity values within a span and re-label. Does this help generalization?
