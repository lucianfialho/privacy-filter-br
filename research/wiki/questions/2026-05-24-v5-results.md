# v5 results: mixed-rewriter training fixes distribution shift (2026-05-24)

## TL;DR

v5 trained on the **union of v3's Haiku-rewritten dataset and v4's gpt-5-nano-rewritten dataset** (~100k examples). The mixed-rewriter approach hypothesized in the [[2026-05-24-v4-postmortem]] worked: v5 achieves F1 0.9912 on Haiku-style holdout AND F1 0.9992 on gpt5nano-style holdout. Spread of 0.0080 vs v4's 0.4458 (55× smaller). v5 is now the production default; **already published to HF Hub**, Space updated, `br-pii-guardrail` lib bumped to 0.1.3.

## Hypothesis tested

Stated in [[2026-05-24-v4-postmortem]]:

> **Diverse rewriter mix:** combine Haiku output (v3 dataset) + gpt-5-nano output (v4 dataset). Train on union → model can't overfit to one style.

Result: confirmed empirically.

## Training details

- **Dataset:** `dataset_br_v5.jsonl` = concat of `dataset_br_v3.jsonl` (49842) + `dataset_br_v4.jsonl` (49900) = 99742 examples
- **Holdout:** `dataset_br_v5_holdout.jsonl` = concat of v3 holdout (4929) + v4 holdout (4964) = 9893 examples
- **Base model:** `neuralmind/bert-base-portuguese-cased` (BERTimbau)
- **Hardware:** Remote box `lucian@lucian-desktop`, RTX 2070 SUPER
- **Hyperparams:** epochs=3, batch=8, grad_accum=2, lr=3e-5, max_length=256 (same as v3/v4)
- **Training time:** ~3h45min wall-time (18702 total steps at ~1.34 it/s)
- **Best checkpoint:** epoch 3 (F1 0.99550 micro)

No new dataset generation needed. Cost: $0 (used existing v3+v4 data already on disk).

## What v5 does NOT fix (known limits)

We tested v5 on the input that exposed v4's failure ("Pedido ML-2024-789456 do cliente CUST-998877. ... faturou R$ 50.000,00 ..."). v5 makes the **same two mistakes as v3 and v4**:

1. **CUST-998877 → labeled as `private_order_id` (should be `private_customer_id`)**
2. **R$ 50.000,00 → not detected (should be `private_client_revenue`)**

These are **template coverage gaps**, not model architecture or data-style issues:

- CUST-XXX and ML-XXX have similar structural patterns and templates put both in "Pedido X / Cliente X" contexts. Model can't learn semantic disambiguation without more diverse context examples.
- Revenue in free-form sentences ("faturou R$ X em outubro") is rare in our templates (revenue appears in tables/dashboards primarily).

**Comparison vs v3 on this input:** v3 fragmented CUST-998877 into 5 spans with mixed labels and low confidence (max 0.87). v5 consolidated it into 1 coherent span with high confidence (0.93+). The bug type changed from "uncertainty" to "confident-but-wrong" — still wrong, but more predictable.

These are addressable in **v6 with template improvements + Phase 1 real-world test set**.

## Lessons reinforced

1. **Single-rewriter monoculture is a real risk.** v4 demonstrated empirically what LAMBADA warned about theoretically.
2. **Cross-holdout is cheap insurance.** ~30 min of CPU was the difference between shipping a buggy model (v4) and shipping a correct one (v5).
3. **Synthetic-to-real gap is its own axis.** v5 fixes synthetic-to-synthetic distribution shift. It does NOT fix synthetic-to-real (CUST/revenue failures still happen).
4. **Templates are a separate optimization frontier.** All three models (v3, v4, v5) fail in similar ways on out-of-template patterns. Next iteration should diversify templates, not just rewriters.
